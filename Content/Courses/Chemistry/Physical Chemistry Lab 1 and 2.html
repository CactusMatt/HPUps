Final Exam

Attendance is compulsory. Depending on enrollment, the class is subdivided into groups of two or three students ("partners") who work together on the same experiments during the whole semester.

Before starting a new experiment, (i) the written report on the preceding experiment must be turned in, and (ii) you and your partner(s) must pass pretest in the lab at the beginning of the lab period after the lecture portion.  You are required to know how to carry out the experiment, the principles of the apparatus you will be using, and the basic theory and equations you will apply in calculating the results.

Before starting an experiment you will have to clean the equipment, if necessary, and take inventory.

1)      The measurements should be performed carefully and safely. Meticulous notes of the primary data obtained should be kept in a notebook, individually by each partner. Procedures actually followed should be described in your laboratory notebook. Do not rely on  a summary  written  before  the  lab.   List lab partners.   Include all experimental observations, data and calculations; you may staple or tape in computer printouts and spectra.  If data is stored in computer files accurately record the data file names and location.  Goggles  and appropriate clothing (no sandals or shorts) must be worn at all times.  Failure to wear safety goggles may result in ejection from lab and an F in the course. In your notebook record a brief outline of the procedure you expect to follow and construct two tables of information: 1) reagents; 2) equipment.

a.        In the reagents table write the chemical name, chemical formula, a drawing of the structure, the state in which it will be found (solid, liquid, gas, or in solution), and hazard information from the Material Safety Data Sheet(s), which will be available.  If solutions are to be prepared, calculate the amounts needed and their concentrations.

b.       Each row in the equipment table should contain the property to be measured, the equipment used for the measurement, and the sample(s) that will be measured.

After finishing an experiment, you must clean all equipment (including glassware) and have the instructor check their condition. Each student is responsible for the equipment he/she is using. Breakage of equipment should be reported immediately to the instructor.

Reports

The part of an experiment involving calculation, organization, and interpretation of the results must be the work of each individual student and must show his/her understanding and competency.   Violation of this rule may result in a zero score for the report.  The format of this part of an experiment is the lab-report. A report should consist of four sections:

1)      Introduction: (~ ½ page) Brief description of the purpose of the experiment; include a schematic drawing of the apparatus or material used. Specify the chemical system investigated, date of experiment, lab partner(s) and title .

2)      Experimental: Presentation of the primary data in a logical sequence, taken from the notebook. They must be reorganized and tabulated or plotted in graphical form, if applicable. Specify what was measured (e.g., time, temperature, etc.).

3)       Calculations and results: Presentation of the equations used. Also, include the most important steps of your numerical calculations, and the computer program used if any. Pay great attention to units and to significant figures. The final result(s) should be reported as "<X> ± s unit," and marked conspicuously. Cite the source of numerical values of quantities if you took them from the literature (author, title, publisher, place, date, page).

Give the uncertainties in the results: Analysis of errors in the measured and derived data. Show quantitatively how the uncertainties of your primary data affect the final results. Compare your results with accepted values taken from the literature, and show the relative error.  Cite the source of numerical values of quantities if you took them from the literature (author, title, publisher, place, date, page).

4)      Draw a conclusion.  Discuss the significance of the experimental results.

Safety Regulations 

The wearing of safety glasses and lab aprons is mandatory in the lab, and no eating or drinking is allowed. The laboratory is equipped with fire extinguishers. Memorize the locations of the emergency shower and eye washer in the lab. Know where the First aid kit is located and the telephone for emergency cases. Any accidents should be reported immediately to the instructor. Waste chemicals must be collected in designated containers for proper disposal.

Required Lab Attire:  READ THIS INFORMATION VERY CAREFULLY!!!

You will be exposed to hazardous chemicals in chemistry lab.  Certain personal protective equipment is necessary to protect your body.  You will not be allowed to attend lab if you are in violation of the following rules.  If you are not dressed appropriately, you will need to leave the lab.  All missed lab work will count as zero.

      1. Goggles, gloves, and aprons are provided and are required at all times.

      2. Closed-toe shoes are required at all times.

      3. Long pants are highly recommended.

      4.  Students are expected to arrive for lab on time and stay until the lab session has been dismissed.
